Let's be honest, the last of the good learning games died with Carmen Sandiego and I had to find out why. PopCap games was founded in the year 2000, and we all fell in love with Bejweled, Dynomite, TyperShark, and Bookworm. Along came the first app store in 2008, and the next almost 20 years have been spent clicking around the myriad pay-to-play mini games. Learning games are limited to fast-math and vocabulary apps that reward memorization and quick-recall, but that is not learning. I am nostalgic for games like The Oregon Train and The Island of Dr. Brain.

Working in the forefront of Education Research with the CESE's NGSS-aligned project-based learning invervention, I found the tech was behdind because we were always waiting for the bureaucracy to approve funding. High-tech developments were no longer high-tech by the time the bureaucrazy came around and the content was developed. After spending time researching AI applications in Education, I noticed there was no education. AI just couldn't mimic what years of carefully developed science has already built. But the tech sector does not have enough educators on staff for anyone to know the difference. Hello click-to-win pay now or come back later vocabulary games. It was exhausting trying to explain to an engineering team why student learning math would have to be able to enter math into the app.

And then I met Dr. Gunnink. After our first discussion about the need to mix education into EdTech, I knew we were going to build something together. At some point in the following weeks, I told her "We both have strong personalities, and I know we are going to fight one day. But we are going to build something amazing together one day." She did not have to ask me twice to help design the psychometric platform that will drive what will be the first good learning game in 2 decades.

Inspired by Dr. Gunnink's tenacity and vision, I designed as system that I believe will drive the future of EdTech.

EduMetrix is an ecosystem to connect education researchers and professionals to the EdTech industry, bringing the latest in learning science to a format accesible to the tech industry. Stay tuned for more details.
